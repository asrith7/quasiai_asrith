Quantum Machine Learning for Enhancing Fluid Dynamic Simulations

Leveraging quantum machine learning techniques to enhance fluid dynamic simulations using Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) and their quantum counterparts (Quantum PINNs or QPINNs). The study explores implementations such as attention-enhanced architectures and Physically-Informed Quantum Neural Networks (PI-QNNs).

Objectives

  • Develop and evaluate classical PINNs for fluid dynamics simulations.
  • Design and implement Quantum PINNs (QPINNs) leveraging quantum circuits and hybrid models.
  • Explore attention-enhanced PINNs for improved feature representation.
  • Investigate Physically-Informed Quantum Neural Networks (PI-QNNs).
  • Benchmark the models in terms of accuracy, efficiency, and scalability.

Methodology

This research integrates classical deep learning techniques with quantum computing. We employ:

  • Classical PINNs: Solving Navier-Stokes equations using TensorFlow and PyTorch.
  • Quantum PINNs (QPINNs): Hybrid quantum-classical models using PennyLane and Qiskit.
  • Attention Mechanisms: Enhancing convergence and feature focus.
  • Tensor Networks: Encoding high-dimensional fluid states efficiently.

Quantum Technologies Used

We use various quantum machine learning frameworks to optimize simulations:

  • PennyLane (Quantum Machine Learning)
  • NVIDIA Modulus (Physics-Informed Neural Networks)
  • Quantum computing platforms such as IBM Quantum and Qiskit.
